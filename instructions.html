How It Works

Use the planner to build a lean AI stack you could actually adopt this month.

EasyAIStack is both a planner and a learning resource for small businesses exploring AI adoption. The goal is not to recommend every AI tool that could possibly work. It is to help owners describe their business, compare the tools most likely to fit, and turn those matches into starter, growth, and advanced stack options they could realistically adopt.

AI Stack Definition

What an AI stack actually is

An AI stack for small business is a curated, connected set of artificial intelligence tools and platforms designed to automate, scale, and manage operations like marketing, sales, and productivity. Rather than isolated tools, a cohesive AI stack connects inputs and automated workflows to replace manual tasks, boost productivity, and improve efficiency at a low monthly cost.

Small Business Use

How small businesses use an AI stack

  • Write faster with AI for emails, replies, summaries, proposals, and content drafts.
  • Reduce admin work by connecting forms, bookings, notes, tasks, and follow-up steps.
  • Improve service by speeding up lead response, reminders, meeting cleanup, and customer communication.
  • Build the stack in stages so the business fixes one workflow first before paying for more software.

How This Site Helps

How this planner helps small businesses choose

This site helps small businesses narrow the field. Instead of browsing a giant AI directory, owners can filter by bottleneck, budget, team size, meetings, automation, content, research, and compliance needs. The planner then ranks better-fit tools, groups them into starter, growth, and advanced stacks, and points to a first workflow worth implementing before the business buys more software.

Using the Planner

How to use this site step by step

  • Choose filters that describe the business, especially the biggest bottleneck, budget, and workload.
  • Review the ranked tools and compare the starter, growth, and advanced stack recommendations.
  • Look at the monthly range and best first workflow so you can start with one practical implementation instead of too many tools.
  • Use Browse Tools to compare categories and use Articles to dig deeper into niche examples, budget setups, and business-specific stacks.
  • Use the Platform Integrations page when the business already depends on tools like Shopify, LinkedIn, Google Workspace, Slack, or HubSpot.

About

What this site is for

EasyAIStack helps owners and lean teams compare AI tools by practical fit. Instead of forcing you through thousands of generic listings, it starts with the workload problem first and ranks a smaller set of likely matches.

Stack Model

Think in layers, not one magic app

  • A core assistant layer handles writing, summaries, internal drafts, and day-to-day AI work.
  • An operations layer handles meetings, scheduling, knowledge, or task management.
  • An automation layer moves data between tools so the stack saves time without extra manual copying.
  • An optional growth layer handles content, support, sales, or design when that is the main bottleneck.
  • The planner turns those layers into starter, growth, and advanced recommendations so teams can adopt AI in stages instead of all at once.

Pricing Notes

Budget bands are planning ranges

The cost ranges shown in the planner are meant to help with stack design, not serve as a live quote. Real pricing changes by plan, seat count, add-ons, and contract terms. Use the ranges to stay realistic while you narrow the stack, then verify live pricing before buying.

Workflow Advice

Start with one repeatable automation

Most teams waste time by buying several AI tools at once and not finishing a single useful workflow. The best way to adopt an AI stack is to choose one high-friction process such as lead follow-up, post-meeting cleanup, or content repurposing, then make that workflow work end to end.
